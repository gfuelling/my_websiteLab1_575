Thai Urban Growth

Thailand has increased its rate of urbanization dramatically in the past few. While still low compared to other developed nations, Thailand has over a 50% urbanization rate. This growth is clustered and expected to continue primarily in the Krung Thep area, which is the area around Bangkok. Thailand is undertaking increasingly large infrastructure improvements nationwide, in order to keep up with the growth of these urban centers. Despite this urban growth, Thailand's fertility rate is low and is below the natural replacement rate. Thus, Thailand's population growth is minimal, and the map shows how more and more citizens are moving into urban areas.

One might think that as more people move into the urban centers that agriculture, a staple of the Thai economy for generations, would lose some of its hold on the national economy. This has not happened, as its share of the national GDP has remained stable.
